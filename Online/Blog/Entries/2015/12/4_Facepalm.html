Facepalm

 

    Have you ever been out in public and, as you’re going on your way, you see someone from behind and are absolutely sure it’s a good friend of yours? So, like any good friend, you do something stupid and silly to mess with them--maybe you kick the back of their foot or say something really loud in their direction like “RUN!!” or “OH MY GOODNESS!!” I hope you do, because if not I feel like even more of an idiot for doing what I did today.

    I was driving to my favorite coffeeshop, Bittersweet, to work on some homework. I was coming from my school and was in a procession of cars driven by other people who go to my school, and so there was a blue Mustang in front of me being driven by one of my fellow football players. He was a hilarious kid, always making jokes and acting crazy to get a laugh; every time we pass each other in the hallway we say, very seriously, “Good job,” and nod with severity. He does a fantastic George Bush impression, and before games he BLASTS hard rock and roll on our stereo while jumping up and down, bellowing, and contorting his face with rage.

    I knew it was him. Same head, same hair, and... well, he drives a Mustang, right? I think I’ve seen him get into it before. Yeah, I’m certain it’s him.

    We pull up to a stoplight, him right in front of me. And like any good friend, I start messing with him.

    My lights were off, so I switch them to parking, then full, then parking, off, then parking, then full, then parking, then off, and pause... all the while staring at him in his rear-view mirror. He looks confused, so I charge right on, going insane with my three light settings. This is great! I thought as he started looking around, and I went to full-blast craziness with my brights.

    Immediately my friend wheels around and stares right at me and...

    It’s not him...

    I stopped my lights’ flickering at once, color rising fast and hot to my cheeks. My scalp started tingling as I looked off into the distance, trying to play it off. Oh, it was terrible; I felt so ashamed, so stupid, so ignorant and immature. I looked back into his rear-view mirror and saw that the stranger was smoldering with anger and disdain. He was probably cursing me out; I saw his mouth angrily spit several words that I was content not to hear.

    This red light was eternal. I sat under his bewildered and furious stare for millennia, trying as hard as I could to keep from meeting his stare and all the while making it worse by ignoring him. Finally, green triumphed and we lurched forward. He raced off ahead of me, much to my great joy.

    I’m such an idiot. Man... I felt, and still feel, beyond stupid and inconsiderate. How could I have mistaken him? How could I have messed like that with a complete stranger? He must hate me... I hope he doesn’t call the police.

    Truth is, he’ll forget me in a matter of days. I’ll forget too, but I make this record as something to laugh about in the future. Shame still sits heavy right now though... may it go away soon.
